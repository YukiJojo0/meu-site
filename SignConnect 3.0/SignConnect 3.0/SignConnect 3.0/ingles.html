Higher education for everyone?

Deaf students still face invisible barriers within universities.

Inclusion in higher education is not yet a reality for all.

The lack of accessibility limits learning and
opportunities for thousands of deaf students.

The dropout rate for deaf students in Brazilian higher education is considered high, often exceeding the general average, occurring mostly in the first two years. Although specific annual data is scarce, studies indicate that students with disabilities, including the deaf, have a higher risk of leaving school.

Main issues and causes of dropout:

LESS THAN 1%:
The presence of students with disabilities is still minimal in universities.
REAL ISOLATION:
Without inclusion, many students do not feel part of the university.
NO ACCESS, NO LEARNING:
The lack of Sign Language interpreters prevents classroom understanding.
INVISIBLE BARRIER:
The written language remains a major academic obstacle.

HOW UNIVERSITIES CAN GUARANTEE ACCESSIBILITY FOR THE DEAF

  • Guarantee qualified Sign Language interpreters
  • Train professors in inclusive practices
  • Use assistive technologies in teaching
  • Produce accessible and adapted materials
  • Promote inclusion inside and outside the classroom
